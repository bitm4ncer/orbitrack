ORBEAT — STRATEGY
================================================================================

NOT A MARKETING PLAN. A BEHAVIOUR PLAN.
The tool sells itself through the music made with it.
No announcements. No launch events. No "excited to share."

THE IDENTITY
------------
  Open source groovebox. Free to play. Internet Archive sounds.
  You start with a beat. Not a blank page.
  Made by one person. Not a startup.

  This matters. Say it plainly everywhere.
  "I made this" > "We're thrilled to announce"

THE ONLY METRIC THAT MATTERS BEFORE LAUNCH
-------------------------------------------
  Can a person with no music theory make something
  they want to share within 5 minutes?

  If yes: ship it.
  If no: fix that first.

================================================================================
PHASE 1 — SEED (before launch, 0 users)
================================================================================

  DO
    - Record 3 session videos with musician friends
    - Real sessions. Real music. No feature walkthrough.
    - Someone makes a track. You film it. 60-90 seconds.
    - Post on their accounts, not yours (borrowed audience)
    - Put the link in their bio for a week

  DO NOT
    - Make a "feature overview" video yet
    - Post "coming soon" teases
    - Build a waitlist
    - Announce anything

  GOAL
    - 3 pieces of evidence that real musicians make real music with this

================================================================================
PHASE 2 — DROP (launch)
================================================================================

  Reddit (primary acquisition)
    - r/WeAreTheMusicMakers
    - r/edmproduction
    - r/beatmakers
    - r/webdev (the "I made a thing" post — different audience, still valid)

    The post:
      Title: "I built a browser groovebox with Internet Archive samples — free"
      Body: 2 sentences. Link. Maybe a gif of the orbs spinning.
      Do not explain features. Let people discover.
      Do not cross-post same day. Space by 1 week per sub.

  YouTube (trust channel)
    - 1x "how I made [specific track]" with the tool — your own session
    - 1x tutorial: "make a beat in 3 minutes, no theory"
    - Do NOT make a product overview video as first video.
      Show music first, explain later.

  TikTok / IG Reels
    - Grab the best 30 seconds from musician session videos
    - No voiceover. Just the music and the orbs.
    - Caption: "browser groovebox, link in bio"
    - Post same content on both platforms simultaneously

  ProductHunt
    - Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday
    - Short description. Screenshot + GIF of orbs.
    - Ask musician friends to upvote + leave a genuine comment
    - Do not spam. One launch, do it properly.

  Hacker News
    - "Show HN: I built a groovebox in the browser"
    - Tech-adjacent audience, some will contribute to open source repo
    - Different crowd than music producers — still worth it

================================================================================
PHASE 3 — COMPOUND (after first users)
================================================================================

  The watermark does the work
    - Every shared clip has "Made with Orbeat — orbeat.app"
    - You don't need to post. Your users post.
    - Optimise for this: make export dead simple, make sharing one tap.

  GitHub does the work
    - Open source repo attracts contributors
    - Issues become feature requests from real users
    - Stars are social proof on the landing page

  Session content cadence (sustainable)
    - 1 musician session video per month
    - Reach out to your network. Ask them to make a track, not to "promote"
    - Musicians who enjoy making music with it will make genuine content
    - You don't owe them money. They get content for their own channels.

  Content you make yourself
    - Only when you have something real to say
    - "I added X feature because Y person asked for it" > "New update released"
    - Changelog as storytelling

================================================================================
SAMPLE COMPANY ANGLE (parallel, not primary)
================================================================================

  Pitch: "I'll integrate your sample pack as a free preset in Orbeat.
          Users demo your sounds in an interactive beat maker.
          You get a branded experience + leads."

  Target first: boutique labels in your existing network.
  Not Splice. Not Loopmasters. Someone you can email directly.

  Ask for: one-time integration fee (€500–1500) or rev share on samples sold.
  Deliverable: a named pack in the sample browser, their logo, a link.

  This is B2B on the side. Not the main strategy. One deal validates it.

================================================================================
MONEY
================================================================================

  Free forever to jam.
  Export is free but watermarked.
  €35 once: clean export + unlimited cloud saves (v2).

  Do not put the price everywhere.
  Let people fall in love first.
  The paywall hits at the moment of ownership — when they want to keep
  what they made. That's the right emotional moment to ask for money.

  Gumroad handles payment.
  License key or Supabase flag unlocks features.
  No subscription. No trial timer. No dark patterns.

  50 sales/month = €1,575 net. That's the target.
  It compounds if the watermark works.

================================================================================
WHAT NOT TO DO
================================================================================

  - No Product Hunt countdown timer posts
  - No "I'm building in public" Twitter/X thread (performative)
  - No cold DMs to music influencers
  - No paid ads (too early, wrong audience targeting)
  - No "excited to announce" language ever
  - No feature list on the landing page hero
  - Do not compare to FL Studio or Ableton in any copy
